## ARTICLE 7: CAPITAL ACCOUNTS

### 7.1 Establishment

The Company shall maintain a separate capital account for each Member.

### 7.2 Capital Contributions

Capital contributions shall be credited to each Member's capital account. No Member shall be obligated to make additional capital contributions without their written consent.

### 7.3 Withdrawals

No Member shall withdraw capital from their capital account without 30 days' written notice to all other Members and receipt of written consent from all Members.

---

## ARTICLE 8: PROFIT AND LOSS ALLOCATION

### 8.1 Net Profits

Net Profits of the Company shall be allocated among the Members in proportion to their respective membership interests.

### 8.2 Net Losses

Net Losses of the Company shall be allocated among the Members in proportion to their respective membership interests; provided, however, that no Member shall be allocated losses in excess of their positive capital account balance.

---

## ARTICLE 9: DISTRIBUTIONS

### 9.1 Timing

Distributions of available cash shall be made to Members at such times as the Members may determine, but no less frequently than annually.

### 9.2 Allocation

Distributions shall be made to Members in proportion to their respective membership interests.

### 9.3 Tax Distributions

To the extent the Company has taxable income, the Company shall distribute to each Member no less than the amount necessary to pay the Member's estimated tax liability attributable to the Company's taxable income allocated to such Member, taking into account applicable tax rates.

---

## ARTICLE 10: MANAGEMENT

### 10.1 Member-Managed

The Company shall be managed by its Members. Each Member shall have equal right to participate in the management of the Company.

### 10.2 Decisions

Except as otherwise provided herein, decisions concerning the management of the Company shall be made by Members holding a majority of the membership interests.

### 10.3 Daily Operations

The Members may delegate the daily operations of the Company to a manager or managers, who need not be Members. Any such delegation shall be documented in writing.

### 10.4 Authority

Each Member is an agent of the Company for the purpose of its business. No Member, however, shall have authority to bind the Company in any contract, agreement, or commitment exceeding $5,000 without prior written approval of all Members.

---

## ARTICLE 11: BOOKS AND RECORDS

### 11.1 Maintenance

The Company shall keep true, complete, and accurate books of account and records.

### 11.2 Inspection

Each Member shall have the right to inspect and copy the Company's books and records upon reasonable notice during normal business hours.

### 11.3 Tax Matters

The Company shall file annual tax returns and make elections as determined by the Members.

---

## ARTICLE 12: BANK ACCOUNTS

All funds of the Company shall be deposited in the Company's name in such bank accounts as the Members may designate. Withdrawals therefrom shall require the signature(s) of such Member(s) as the Members may designate.

---

## ARTICLE 13: TRANSFER RESTRICTIONS

### 13.1 Prohibition

No Member shall sell, assign, transfer, pledge, hypothecate, or otherwise dispose of their membership interest, in whole or in part, without first offering such interest to the other Members on the same terms and conditions.

### 13.2 Right of First Refusal

Existing Members shall have a right of first refusal to purchase the membership interest being transferred, in proportion to their respective membership interests.

### 13.3 Permitted Transfers

Notwithstanding the foregoing, a Member may transfer their membership interest to an immediate family member (spouse, child, parent, or sibling) without triggering the right of first refusal, provided that such transferee agrees in writing to be bound by the terms of this Agreement.

---

## ARTICLE 14: DISSOCIATION

A Member may dissociate from the Company upon 30 days' written notice to all other Members.

---

## ARTICLE 15: DISSOLUTION AND WINDING UP

### 15.1 Events Triggering Dissolution

The Company shall be dissolved upon:
- The unanimous written consent of all Members
- The entry of a decree of judicial dissolution
- The dissociation of a Member if the remaining Members do not unanimously agree to continue the Company within 90 days
- As otherwise required by law

### 15.2 Winding Up

Upon dissolution, the Members shall:
- Wind up the Company's business and affairs
- Collect all Company assets
- Pay or make provision for all Company debts and liabilities
- Distribute the remaining assets first to Members in proportion to their positive capital account balances, then to Members in proportion to their membership interests

### 15.3 Timing

Winding up shall be completed as expeditiously as possible.

---

## ARTICLE 16: INDEMNIFICATION

The Company shall indemnify and hold harmless each Member from and against any and all claims, demands, losses, damages, liabilities, costs, and expenses arising out of or related to the Company's business, to the fullest extent permitted by Minnesota law.

---

## ARTICLE 17: AMENDMENTS

This Agreement may be amended, modified, or supplemented only by a written instrument executed by Members holding at least a majority of the membership interests; provided, however, that any amendment that adversely affects a Member's economic interest in a materially disproportionate way shall require that Member's written consent.

---
