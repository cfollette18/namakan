---

## ARTICLE 1 - DEFINITIONS

1.1 **"Confidential Information"** means any non-public information disclosed by one Party (the "Disclosing Party") to the other Party (the "Receiving Party"), whether orally, in writing, electronically, or by inspection, including but not limited to:

- Business plans, strategies, financial information, and projections
- Customer and supplier lists, pricing information
- Technical data, trade secrets, know-how, research, and development
- Product plans, designs, and specifications
- Software, algorithms, and source code
- Marketing strategies and materials
- Personnel information
- AI systems, prompts, frameworks, and outputs

1.2 **Exclusions.** Confidential Information does not include information that:

(a) Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party;
(b) Was rightfully known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure;
(c) Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of Confidential Information;
(d) Is lawfully obtained from a third party who has the right to disclose it;
(e) Is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or court order, provided the Receiving Party gives prompt written notice to allow the Disclosing Party to seek protective measures.

---

## ARTICLE 2 - OBLIGATIONS

2.1 **Use Restrictions.** The Receiving Party shall:
- Use Confidential Information solely for the Purpose
- Not use Confidential Information for any other purpose without prior written consent
- Not reverse engineer, decompile, or disassemble any tangible items containing Confidential Information

2.2 **Disclosure Restrictions.** The Receiving Party shall:
- Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent
- Limit disclosure to employees, contractors, and advisors who have a need to know
- Ensure those who receive Confidential Information are bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those in this Agreement
- Take reasonable measures to protect the confidentiality of the information

2.3 **Permitted Disclosures.** Notwithstanding Section 2.2, disclosure is permitted to:
- Employees and contractors with a need to know, who are bound by confidentiality obligations
- Professional advisors (attorneys, accountants) who are bound by professional obligations
- As required by law, with proper notice to the Disclosing Party

## ARTICLE 3 - OWNERSHIP

3.1 **Retention of Rights.** Each Party retains all right, title, and interest in and to its own Confidential Information. Nothing in this Agreement transfers ownership of any Confidential Information.

3.2 **No License.** Nothing in this Agreement grants either Party any license or right to use the other's Confidential Information except as necessary to further the Purpose.
