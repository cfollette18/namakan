# Namakan AI Consulting — Discovery Call Script
## Custom AI Engineering | 30–45 Minute Discovery Calls

> **Purpose:** This call is NOT a sales call. It is a structured discovery conversation to understand the client's proprietary data assets, operational workflows, and where custom AI could create meaningful business impact.
>
> **Goal:** Earn the right to propose a paid technical assessment ($1,500–$2,000), which positions us for the full custom AI engineering project.
>
> **Success metric:** They feel understood. They want the technical assessment. They see you as a peer, not a vendor.
>
> **Your role:** Chief listening officer. You talk 30%, they talk 70%.

---

## 🗓️ Before the Call

- [ ] Confirm call via email with calendar invite
- [ ] Research the company: products, services, recent news, leadership
- [ ] Research the contact: their role, background, recent work
- [ ] Identify 2–3 specific proprietary data assets they likely have
- [ ] Prepare 3 specific AI opportunity hypotheses
- [ ] Send reminder email 24 hours before with agenda
- [ ] Join call 2 minutes early with video on (if video call)

---

## 📞 OPENING (Minutes 0–3)

**Goal:** Build rapport. Set the frame. Get them talking.

### Greeting:
> "Hi [Name], thanks for making time for this. How's [their day/week/quarter] going?"

*(If they seem busy)*

> "I'll keep this efficient — I know your time is valuable. My goal today is just to understand your situation well enough to know whether there's a real opportunity here. No pitch, no pressure. If there isn't, I'll tell you that."

### Agenda Setting:
> "Here's what I'd like to cover in the next 30 minutes or so:
> 1. What your current data and workflows look like
> 2. Where you see the biggest gaps or inefficiencies
> 3. Where you've tried AI before — what worked, what didn't
> 4. Whether a custom approach could make sense for your situation
>
> Sound good? Anything you'd add?"

---

## 🔍 PART 1: Understanding Their World (Minutes 3–12)

**Goal:** Learn their business through their eyes. Identify proprietary data assets. Understand their workflows.

### Opening Question:
> "To start — can you give me a quick overview of how [Company] operates? Not the website version — the real version. What are the key things that actually have to happen every day to keep the business running?"

**Listen for:** Proprietary processes, domain-specific knowledge, institutional expertise, key data assets.

### Data Discovery Questions:

> "You mentioned [something they said]. I'm curious — what data do you generate or collect that reflects that?"

> "Do you have any historical records — past cases, projects, outcomes, transactions — that you'd consider proprietary institutional knowledge?"

> "Where does your team's expertise live right now? Is it in their heads, in documents, in software systems?"

> "What information do your people spend time looking for that should be easier to find?"

**Key ICP Signals to Listen For:**
- Proprietary datasets (customer interactions, domain-specific outcomes, historical decisions)
- Complex workflows with judgment calls (not just data entry)
- Institutional knowledge that walks out the door with employees
- Repeated decisions where context matters

### Workflow Deep Dive:

> "Walk me through how a [specific process] actually works — the real steps, not the theoretical ones."

**Probe:**
> "Where does that process typically break down?"

> "How long does that take today? How many people touch it?"

> "What's the cost of a mistake in that process?"

---

## 🔍 PART 2: AI History + Pain Points (Minutes 12–22)

**Goal:** Understand their AI experience. Identify their real pain. Assess urgency and receptiveness.

### AI Experience Questions:

> "Have you experimented with AI or machine learning in your operations? What have you tried?"

**If YES:**
> "What did you try? What worked well and what didn't?"

> "Did you use generic tools, or did anyone try to customize them to your data?"

> "If it didn't work — what do you think went wrong?"

> "What would have to be true for AI to actually move the needle for you?"

**If NO:**
> "What's held you back from exploring it? Budget, uncertainty about where it would help, data readiness?"

> "Do you have a sense of where AI could theoretically help — even if you haven't pulled the trigger?"

### Pain Prioritization:

> "If we could wave a magic wand and solve one AI-related problem for [Company], what would you choose?"

> "Is this something keeping you up at night, or more of a 'would be nice' opportunity?"

> "If we could show you a meaningful pilot in 4–6 weeks — something that actually worked on your data — would that be worth exploring?"

---

## 🔍 PART 3: Budget + Authority + Timeline (Minutes 22–30)

**Goal:** Qualify on commercial readiness. Don't pitch price yet — understand their context.

### Budget Framing:
> "I don't want to get ahead of the assessment, but I do want to understand your situation — if we found a meaningful AI opportunity, what's the budget context you'd be working within? Is this a case where you'd need to build a business proposal, or do you have latitude to move on something like this?"

**Probe if vague:**
> "For context — custom AI engineering projects in your space typically range from $15K–$75K depending on scope and complexity. Is that in the ballpark of what you'd be considering?"

### Decision Process:
> "If we got to the end of an assessment and found a real opportunity — what would the approval process look like?"

> "Who else would be involved in a decision like this?"

> "Are there any constraints I should know about — procurement processes, vendor approval requirements, data restrictions?"

### Timeline:
> "What does your timeline look like? Are you actively evaluating this now, or is this more of a 'someday/maybe' conversation?"

> "Is there a fiscal year or planning cycle we should be aware of?"

---

## 🎯 PART 4: The Custom AI Framing (Minutes 30–38)

**Goal:** Connect what they said to the custom AI engineering model. Show them you listened. Plant the seed for the assessment.

> "Based on everything you've shared, here's what I'm hearing — and tell me if I'm off."

> "You have [proprietary data asset]. Most generic AI tools can't access that context — they don't know your patients/patients/operations/market the way your team does."

> "What I'm envisioning is something built specifically on that data — so the AI doesn't just know about [their domain] in general, it knows about [Company's specific approach, history, and context]."

> "The result would be something that [specific outcome they described wanting] — not a chatbot that answers generic questions, but a system that actually understands your domain and does [specific task] in your context."

> "Does that resonate?"

### Objection Anticipation:

**If they seem skeptical about AI value:**
> "I hear you — there's a lot of hype. The difference between generic AI and custom AI is the difference between a general contractor who read about your house plans and an architect who spent a week inside your building. The context matters enormously."

**If they seem worried about implementation complexity:**
> "That's a real concern, and I won't pretend custom AI engineering is plug-and-play. But the way we work is: we do a focused technical assessment first — 2–3 weeks, $1,500–$2,000 — that tells us whether the data is ready and whether the opportunity is real. We don't build anything until we know it's viable."

**If they seem excited and want to skip to the proposal:**
> "I appreciate the enthusiasm — and I want to make sure we build the right thing. Before we propose anything, we need to understand your data quality, your technical environment, and whether a custom approach will actually outperform what you're doing now. That's what the assessment is for."

---

## 📋 PART 5: Next Steps (Minutes 38–45)

**Goal:** Close on a specific next step — the paid technical assessment.

### Proposal Framing:
> "Here's where I'd suggest we go from here — and this is a real next step, not a vague 'let's stay in touch.'"

> "I'd like to propose a focused **Technical Assessment** for [Company]. Over 2–3 weeks, we'd:
> 1. Audit your [specific data asset] — assess quality, structure, and readiness
> 2. Identify the top 2–3 custom AI opportunities specific to your workflows
> 3. Build a proof-of-concept on one of those opportunities using your actual data
> 4. Deliver a clear recommendation — what to build, what it would cost, what it would deliver
>
> The investment is $[1,500–2,000], and it typically takes 2–3 weeks. If we find something viable, we'd then scope the full project together."

### Handling "Can you do it for free?":
> "I understand — and I appreciate that you've given this conversation time. The reason we charge for the assessment is that we're doing real technical work — data analysis, POC building — not just a slide deck. The assessment is scoped to give you a real answer, not a pitch. If we find there's no viable opportunity here, I'd rather tell you that than sell you on something that won't work."

### Close:
> "Does that approach make sense for you? If so, I can send over a brief scope document and we'll get started. If not — I'd rather hear why than assume."

---

## 🚫 Objection Handling Reference

### "We already have an AI strategy / AI vendor"
> "That's helpful context. Most companies at your level do. What I'm proposing is complementary — we're not replacing an AI strategy, we're building custom systems on top of your proprietary data that generic tools and vendors can't access. Think of it as the layer that's uniquely yours. Would it be useful to compare notes on where that gap might exist in your current approach?"

### "We need to think about data security / HIPAA / compliance"
> "That's exactly why we do a technical assessment before we propose anything — to understand your data environment and constraints. We've worked in [regulated environments] before. Compliance isn't a blocker for us — it's a design constraint we engineer around. Happy to walk through our approach in detail if you want."

### "We tried AI and it didn't work"
> "That's actually really useful to know. Can I ask — what did you try, and where specifically did it fall short? [Listen carefully.] That helps me understand whether the issue was the technology, the implementation, or the data — because those are three very different problems with very different solutions."

### "Can you give me a ballpark before the assessment?"
> "I can give you a rough range based on what you've described — somewhere between $[15K–$75K] for a full project. But I'd rather not quote numbers until we've looked at your data quality and workflow specifics. The last thing I want to do is quote a number that's wrong for your situation. The assessment is designed to give you a precise number."

### "I need to loop in my boss / team / IT"
> "Absolutely — that makes sense. I'd suggest we do the same thing I proposed — a technical assessment — but with a broader audience. We can include whoever you think should be in the room. The assessment output is designed to be shared — it gives your stakeholders a clear picture of the opportunity without requiring deep AI expertise to evaluate."
