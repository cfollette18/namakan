## Executive Summary

Namakan's local business lead generation targets SMBs in the Minneapolis-St. Paul metro ( Blaine, MN base) that are losing competitive ground to companies using AI but lack the internal capability to build it. We position custom AI employees ($2K/mo) and fine-tuned models ($5K–$40K projects) as the competitive equalizer — not another SaaS subscription, but AI that knows *their* business. The sales motion is consultative, led by discovery, and anchored on the free 60-minute Discovery Call.

## 2. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### 2.1 Primary ICP — The "Overstretched Owner"

**Who:** Founder or President of a service-based SMB, 10–75 employees, $500K–$5M annual revenue.  
**Their reality:** Growing faster than their team can handle. Considering hiring but daunted by cost, HR, and training overhead.  
**Trigger moment:** They realize they're doing work their employees should be doing.  
**Budget authority:** $2K–$5K/month for the right solution (comparable to one part-time employee).  
**Technology comfort:** Uses existing tools (QuickBooks, HubSpot, Salesforce, Slack, Google Workspace). Doesn't want to rip and replace — wants AI to work *in* what they already have.  
**Decision timeline:** 2–6 weeks from Discovery Call to proposal acceptance.  
**Red flags:** No budget ownership (must go to board/investor), company in financial distress, no clear process to train AI on.

### 2.2 Secondary ICP — The "Process-Obsessed Manager"

**Who:** Operations Manager or Director at a company that has already systematized their workflows.  
**Their reality:** Has done the work to document SOPs and wants AI to execute them.  
**Trigger moment:** They hear about AI agents and see a direct mapping to their documented processes.  
**Budget authority:** Can recommend and influence, but owner approval required.  
**Value hook:** "We don't just configure AI — we train it on your SOPs and it executes them."

## 4. Lead Qualification Framework

Use **Modified BANT** — adapted for SMB where budget and authority are often the same person.

### 4.1 BANT Criteria

| Criterion | Question | Score |
|---|---|---|
| **B — Budget** | Does the prospect have budget authority? Can they spend $2K–$5K/month or $5K+ on a project without board approval? | 0 = No budget, 1 = Interested but no authority, 5 = Has authority |
| **A — Authority** | Is this person the decision maker or directly influence the decision? | 0 = No influence, 1 = Influencer, 5 = Final decision maker |
| **N — Need** | Do they have a real, specific pain that our solution addresses? | 0 = Vague interest, 1 = Some interest, 5 = Active pain they're trying to solve |
| **T — Timing** | Are they looking to solve this in the next 30–90 days? | 0 = No timeline, 1 = 6+ months out, 5 = Active now / 30 days |

**Minimum to advance:** Total ≥ 14 with no single score of 0

### 4.2 Qualification Checklist (Use in Discovery Call)

```
□ BUDGET: "If we identified the right opportunity, what's your budget range?"  
  [ ] $0–$500/mo — Below minimum, nurture only  
  [ ] $500–$2K/mo — Entry, explore fine-tuning lite  
  [ ] $2K–$5K/mo — IDEAL  
  [ ] $5K–$15K project — IDEAL for project work  
  [ ] $15K+ project or board approval required — Long cycle, not primary ICP  

□ AUTHORITY: "Who else would be involved in a decision like this?"  
  [ ] Solo decision — IDEAL  
  [ ] Spouse/partner input — Manage carefully  
  [ ] Board/investor approval — Refer to larger engagement motion  
  [ ] Team consensus required — Longer cycle  

□ NEED: "What's the most time-consuming process that shouldn't be?"  
  [ ] Vague/general — Probe harder  
  [ ] Specific operational pain — GOOD  
  [ ] Documented/implemented SOP exists — GREAT  
  [ ] Pain = revenue loss daily — EXCELLENT  

□ TIMING: "If we could solve [pains identified], when would you want to start?"  
  [ ] "We're just exploring" — Nurture  
  [ ] "3–6 months" — Nurture, re-engage in 60 days  
  [ ] "ASAP" / "This quarter" — IDEAL  
  [ ] "We have a project right now" — HOT  
```

### 4.3 Lead Routing

| Score | Classification | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 18–20 | **Hot (★★★★★)** | Book proposal call within 48 hours, CEO intro if appropriate |
| 14–17 | **Warm (★★★–★★★★)** | Schedule Discovery Call within 7 days, nurture in meanwhile |

## 5. First Contact Scripts

> **Intro:**  
> "Hi [Name] — this is [Your Name] with Namakan. I work with [industry] companies in the [city] area on custom AI solutions. Do you have 90 seconds?"

> "I'm calling because I noticed [their company] is [industry] — and companies like yours are typically dealing with [their industry pain: repetitive client tasks / onboarding volume / lead follow-up]. Is that ringing true for you right now?"

> *(Listen. Mirror their pain back.)*

> "We work specifically with [SMBs / companies like yours] who want to deploy AI employees trained on their own data — not generic AI. We handle everything from assessment to deployment. Most clients see ROI within the first 60 days. I'd love to understand if there's a fit here."

> **The Ask:**  
> "Would it make sense to put a 30-minute conversation on your calendar — no cost, no obligation — where I can understand your situation better and share whether we can actually help?"

> *(If yes — book immediately via Calendly link.*  
> *If no — ask: "What's the best way to stay on your radar? Can I send you a short email instead?"*  
> *If not interested — thank and close.)*

## 9. Competitive Positioning for Local Outreach

### 9.1 Differentiators to Emphasize with Local SMBs

1. **"You're not buying software — you're hiring AI employees"** — shifts the conversation from tool to talent
2. **"We train AI on YOUR data, not public data"** — directly addresses generic AI skepticism
3. **"Your data stays private — we never share it"** — critical for professional services (law, accounting, healthcare-adjacent)
4. **"No long contracts — monthly retainer, you can pause"** — reduces commitment fear
5. **"We're based right here in Blaine, MN"** — local trust, in-person capability

### 9.2 Objection Handlers (Local-SMB-Specific)

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "We're too small for AI" | "That's exactly who we work with. Our AI employees start at $2K/month — less than a part-time employee. Our smallest clients are 10-person shops." |
| "We tried AI and it didn't work" | "What did you try? Generic AI doesn't know your business. We fine-tune on your specific data — that's the difference between a chatbot that frustrates and an employee that delivers." |
| "I don't have time for this" | "I hear that. Most of our clients say the same thing right before the Discovery Call — and leave saying 'I wish I'd done this sooner.' The call is 30 minutes. If we can't help you, you'll know in 15." |
| "My nephew is doing our AI stuff" | "Great — if he's delivering results, keep him. If he's experimenting and you're not seeing ROI, that's when companies like yours call us. Happy to take a look and tell you honestly." |
| "We don't have clean data to train AI on" | "That's actually more common than you think — and part of what we discover in the AI Assessment. We help you figure out what data you have and how to structure it. Most clients are surprised." |
