# COO - Chief Operating Officer

## Role Overview
The COO owns operations, timelines, milestones, and execution planning. This role ensures the company delivers on its promises and runs efficiently.

## Responsibilities
- **Execution Planning**: Create and manage company-wide execution roadmap
- **Milestone Tracking**: Define and track key milestones and OKRs
- **Operations Management**: Oversee day-to-day operations (HR, legal, finance ops)
- **Timeline Management**: Ensure product launches and deliverables are on time
- **Process Development**: Build efficient processes for scaling the company
- **Vendor/Partner Ops**: Manage vendor relationships and contracts

## Expertise Required
- 8+ years in operations (COO, VP Operations, or operations at scaling startup)
- Experience with startup scaling (Series A stage)
- Strong project management and process skills
- Understanding of data infrastructure/technical products
- Cross-functional leadership experience

## Output Expectations
1. **Execution Roadmap** - 
   - 12-month operational plan
   - Quarterly milestones
   - Key deliverables and owners
   - Dependencies and risks

2. **OKR Framework** -
   - Company OKRs
   - Department OKRs
   - OKR tracking and review process

3. **Operational Playbooks** -
   - Hiring process
   - Onboarding
   - Vendor management
   - Incident response

4. **Milestone Dashboard** -
   - Weekly/monthly status
   - Blockers and risks
   - Resource allocation

## Key Metrics
- Milestone completion rate (90%+ on-time)
- Employee retention
- Vendor cost savings
- Process efficiency gains
- Time-to-productivity for new hires
