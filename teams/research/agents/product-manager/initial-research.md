# Research Lead Deliverable: Problem/Opportunity Framework

## Problem Statement

The data cloud market (~$40B TAM) is dominated by incumbents (Snowflake, Databricks, BigQuery) who have significant weaknesses that remain unaddressed:

1. **Cost Complexity**: Customers are blindsided by unpredictable bills despite "reading documentation"
2. **AI as Afterthought**: AI capabilities are bolt-on, not core to the architecture
3. **False Multi-Cloud**: Platforms are available on multiple clouds but don't offer seamless cross-cloud experience
4. **Poor Developer Experience**: SQL-only interfaces, limited APIs, no modern tooling
5. **Data Quality Gap**: No native data quality - always an afterthought requiring separate tools

## Target Customers

**Primary**: Mid-market companies (200-2000 employees)
- Growing data needs but limited teams
- Frustrated with complexity of enterprise solutions
- Want "enterprise-grade without enterprise complexity"

**Secondary**: Enterprise data teams
- Dissatisfied with current costs and complexity
- Looking for alternatives to Snowflake/Databricks
- Want better AI capabilities

## Revolutionary Differentiation

What makes this "revolutionary" vs. incremental improvement:

1. **AI-Native Architecture**: Not adding AI to existing platform, but building platform where AI is fundamental
2. **Self-Optimizing**: Platform uses AI to optimize itself (storage, compute, quality)
3. **True Universal**: One platform for all data types, all clouds, all use cases
4. **Developer Joy**: Treat data platform like a modern developer tool

## Hypothesis

A new data cloud platform that:
- Puts AI at the core
- Simplifies complexity that frustrates users
- Offers true multi-cloud experience
- Prioritizes developer experience
- Has transparent, predictable pricing

...can capture significant market share from incumbents by addressing their fundamental weaknesses.
