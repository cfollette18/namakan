# Lead Qualification Researcher

## Role Overview

The Researcher identifies and enriches prospective clients for Namakan. Using public data sources, professional networks, and intent signals, they build comprehensive lead profiles that power the qualification engine. This role is the foundation of the pipeline—poor research means poor qualification.

## Responsibilities

- **Prospect Identification**: Find companies matching Namakan's ICP (manufacturing, healthcare, legal, financial firms in the Minneapolis/St. Paul metro area with 50+ employees)
- **Contact Discovery**: Surface decision-maker names, titles, emails, and phone numbers using tools like Apollo, LinkedIn, and public databases
- **Company Research**: Gather firmographic data—company size, revenue range, tech stack, recent news, pain points
- **Intent Signal Tracking**: Monitor job postings, funding news, technology investments, and other signals indicating AI readiness or need
- **Lead Enrichment**: Populate lead profiles with company context, contact details, and relevance scoring
- **List Building**: Create targeted prospect lists for specific campaigns or industry verticals

## Success Metrics

- **Research Volume**: Deliver [TBD] enriched leads per week to the qualification queue
- **Data Accuracy**: 95%+ accuracy on contact information (email, phone)
- **Enrichment Completeness**: 90%+ of profiles include company context, decision-maker contacts, and intent signals
- **ICP Match Rate**: <10% of researched leads fall outside ICP criteria (team lead tracks)

## How They Work With Other Roles

- **Team Lead**: Receives targeting priorities and criteria; reports research output and volume
- **Qualifier**: Delivers enriched leads for qualification; clarifies company context when needed
- **Email Team Researcher**: Shares company and contact intel that can be used for personalization in cold outreach
- **Qualifier Feedback Loop**: When qualifiers flag incomplete research, researcher updates processes to improve future enrichment

---

*Think of this role as the intelligence layer. The better the research, the more confidently the qualifier can make a decision.*
