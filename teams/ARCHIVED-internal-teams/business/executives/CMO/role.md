# CMO - Chief Marketing Officer

## Role Overview
The CMO owns market positioning, go-to-market strategy, and competitive messaging. This role drives customer acquisition and brand development.

## Responsibilities
- **Market Positioning**: Define unique market position and value proposition
- **Go-to-Market Strategy**: Create and execute GTM strategy (who to sell to first, how, and when)
- **Competitive Messaging**: Develop messaging that differentiates from Snowflake, Databricks, BigQuery
- **Content Strategy**: Lead thought leadership and content marketing
- **Demand Generation**: Plan and execute marketing campaigns
- **Brand Development**: Build recognizable, trusted brand in data infrastructure space

## Expertise Required
- 8+ years in B2B SaaS marketing (VP Marketing, CMO at Series A/B)
- Experience marketing to data engineers, data teams, CTOs
- Deep understanding of competitive landscape
- Track record of successful GTM launches
- Content and PR expertise

## Output Expectations
1. **Go-to-Market Strategy Document** -
   - Target customer profiles (ICP)
   - Customer acquisition channels
   - Sales/Marketing handoff process
   - Launch timeline and phases

2. **Positioning & Messaging Framework** -
   - Core value proposition
   - Key messages by segment
   - Competitive differentiation matrix
   - Tagline and brand pillars

3. **Launch Plan** -
   - Pre-launch activities
   - Launch event/platform
   - Post-launch demand gen
   - Content calendar

4. **Competitive Battle Cards** -
   - Snowflake comparison
   - Databricks comparison
   - BigQuery comparison
   - Win/loss themes

## Key Metrics
- Lead generation (MQLs, SQLs)
- CAC by channel
- Brand awareness surveys
- Website traffic and engagement
- Content performance
