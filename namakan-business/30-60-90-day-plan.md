# Namakan AI — 30/60/90 Day Action Plan

## Positioning: CUSTOM AI ENGINEERING FIRM

**We build bespoke AI systems for each client — fine-tuned models, RAG pipelines, agentic workflows, and Custom AI Employees — trained on their data, integrated into their systems, designed for their domain.**

**Core Positioning Statement:**
"We engineer custom AI systems for businesses with proprietary data. Not pre-built agents. Not generic tools. AI built from the ground up for your specific data, your domain, your workflows."

**The Pitch:**
> "We build AI that knows your business — not generic AI, but AI trained on YOUR data, integrated into YOUR systems, solving YOUR specific problems."

## 📅 Days 1–30: Foundation & First Conversations

### Brand & Positioning
- [ ] Update website and all marketing materials to "Custom AI Engineering" positioning — NO mention of "pre-built agents" or "off-the-shelf AI"
- [ ] Refresh LinkedIn profile and company page to reflect custom engineering firm positioning
- [ ] Update email signature, business cards, and all external materials
- [ ] Create one-pager or PDF leave-behind: "What We Build vs. What AI Tool Vendors Sell"
- [ ] **Messaging audit:** Every piece of content should say "custom," "bespoke," or "engineered" — never "agent" (unless referring to our deliverable as a component)

### Tools & Operations
- [ ] Document engineering methodology: how we approach fine-tuning, RAG, and agentic workflows
- [ ] Set up data handling protocols: how we isolate and protect client proprietary data
- [ ] Create proposal templates for each service line (Fine-Tuning, RAG, Workflow, AI Employee)
- [ ] Build assessment template: the structured intake process before scoping a project
- [ ] Set up contract templates (Master Service Agreement + Statement of Work per engagement)
- [ ] Financial tracking: how to price and invoice custom engineering projects

### Target Market & Outreach
- [ ] Identify 15–20 ideal-fit companies: $5M–$100M revenue, heavy proprietary data, decision-maker frustration with generic AI
- [ ] Research target verticals: legal, healthcare, financial services, manufacturing, professional services
- [ ] Build target list with: company name, decision-maker name, LinkedIn, company context (why they'd need custom AI)
- [ ] **Script update:** All outreach now leads with "custom AI engineering" — "We build AI systems for companies with proprietary data — not generic tools, custom systems trained on your data"
- [ ] Send 20–30 personalized outreach messages (LinkedIn or email) — focus on companies with clear proprietary data assets
- [ ] Begin thought leadership content: one LinkedIn post about custom AI vs. generic AI

### Pipeline Goal
- [ ] Target: **3–5 qualified conversations started**
- [ ] Target: **1 discovery call booked**

## 📅 Days 31–60: Land First Engagements

### Client Delivery
- [ ] Conduct 4–6 discovery calls (60 min each) — understand the client's data, domain, and highest-impact opportunity
- [ ] Deliver 2–3 AI Assessments ($2,500 each, applied to project) — structured analysis with specific recommendations
- [ ] Submit 2–3 custom proposals — scoped for fine-tuning, RAG pipeline, or agentic workflow
- [ ] Begin negotiation on first custom engineering engagement

### Pipeline & Referrals
- [ ] Follow up with every lead from Days 1–30 — custom AI conversations often take longer to mature
- [ ] Ask every contact: "Do you know any peers at companies with significant proprietary data who are frustrated with generic AI?"
- [ ] Continue outreach: 20+ new messages to target list
- [ ] Post 3–4 LinkedIn thought leadership posts about custom AI engineering
- [ ] Identify 2–3 potential referral sources: CPAs, lawyers, IT firms, industry consultants

### Sales Cycle Management
- [ ] Track every lead through the pipeline: Discovery → Assessment → Proposal → Contract
- [ ] Expect longer sales cycles (6–12 weeks for custom engineering) — be patient, nurture relationships
- [ ] Refine pricing based on first conversations — do proposals need adjustment?
- [ ] Document common objections: "We already use ChatGPT" → "Generic AI doesn't know your data. We build AI that does."

### Content
- [ ] Draft first case study framework: "How We Identified the AI Opportunity" (anonymized)
- [ ] Write blog post or LinkedIn article: "Why Generic AI Fails Businesses With Proprietary Data"
- [ ] Create email nurture sequence for leads not ready to commit — stay top of mind

### Revenue Goal
- [ ] Target: **1 signed custom AI project by Day 60**
- [ ] Target: **$5,000–$15,000 signed revenue**
- [ ] Target: **2–3 assessments in progress**

## ⚠️ Key Differences From Off-the-Shelf AI Sales

| Off-the-Shelf Agent Sales | Custom AI Engineering |
|--------------------------|------------------------|
| Fast sales cycle (days–weeks) | Longer sales cycle (6–12 weeks) |
| Low ticket ($500–$2K/mo) | High ticket ($5K–$50K/project) |
| Volume: many small clients | Focused: fewer, larger clients |
| Rapid onboarding | Deep discovery required |
| Product configured by client | System engineered for client |
| Client self-serves | We deliver the outcome |

**Key Mindset Shift:** We're not selling subscriptions. We're selling custom engineering projects. The sales process requires more education, more trust-building, and more patience. But the deals are 10x larger.

## ⚠️ Key Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| "We already have ChatGPT" objection | Education: generic AI doesn't know YOUR data; we build AI that does |
| Long sales cycle starving revenue | Maintain pipeline discipline; 5+ conversations always in progress |
| Scope creep on custom projects | Fixed-scope proposals with clear acceptance criteria |
| Client expectations vs. reality | Set milestones, demos, and acceptance criteria upfront |
| Proprietary data security concerns | Documented protocols, isolated environments, clear data handling |
| Technical execution risk | Start with well-scoped projects; build methodology as you learn |
