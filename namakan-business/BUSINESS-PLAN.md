## The Problem

Generic AI fails businesses because:

- **No domain knowledge** — AI doesn't know your products, your industry, your customers
- **Can't access private data** — Your docs, your CRM, your internal knowledge lives everywhere except where the AI can see it
- **Hallucinations cost money** — Generic AI makes up answers about things it shouldn't
- **Off-the-shelf doesn't fit** — Your workflows are unique. Generic tools handle generic cases.
- **Implementation is underestimated** — Most AI projects fail not because of AI, but because no one engineered the system properly

The market is flooded with AI tools. What businesses actually need is AI **engineering**.

## Operations

### Engagement Process

1. **Discovery Call** (30 min) — Map their data sources (CRM, DB, files), understand workflows
2. **Data Assessment** — Audit data volume, quality, PII exposure; define training scope
3. **Proposal** — Fine-tuning tier + optional add-ons (hosting, agents, employees)
4. **Data Connection** — n8n workflow connects to their sources; extract and clean data
5. **Training** — Colab trains the 8B model (~45 min); you get notified
6. **Delivery** — Download the .gguf model file, or host on our infrastructure
7. **Expand** — Add agentic workflows and AI employees as they see value

### Technology Stack

- **Training:** Google Colab (GPU), Qwen2.5-8B base model
- **Fine-Tuning:** LoRA (QLoRA technique), Axolotl
- **Automation:** n8n (data connectors, workflows, webhooks)
- **Data Cleaning:** Custom JavaScript nodes (PII removal, formatting)
- **Agentic:** Custom agents with tool calling, n8n orchestration
- **Hosting:** Dedicated GPU VMs (Lambda Labs, RunPod)
- **Integration:** API-first, webhooks, direct database connections

### Engineering Standards

- Every project starts with data audit and domain analysis
- Private, isolated environments for client data
- Version control on all custom code and prompts
- Reproducible builds — client owns the system when we're done
- Clear documentation and handover packages
